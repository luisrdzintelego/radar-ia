3rd Stop

In this stop, we will learn about one of the elements of the Inclusion Triad: unconscious bias and how it may influence our decisions and behaviors. Recognizing and managing this bias is essential to create a more inclusive and equitable environment.

But what is unconscious bias?

A set of learned attitudes, beliefs, assumptions or stereotypes that exist in our subconscious and can involuntarily influence (unconsciously) the way we think and act.

They are also known as implicit bias or unconscious prejudice.

Mental shortcuts to process information faster.

Automatic association or attitude about characteristics, people, social group or theme.

Powerful predictor of our behavior.

We see the world through our perspective and our unconscious bias. Everyone through different lenses, and that's what makes us human. Through our personal filters, the available information is considerably reduced, and it is that fraction that we use when we form biased judgments.

The following examples show how our perception is shaped by different cognitive biases, highlighting four key elements: perception, preferences, interpretation, and selective attention. Each of these factors shows us how, unconsciously, we focus our attention on certain aspects, while ignoring others that could be equally relevant. This mental filter is not accidental; It is a biological need that allows our brain to process a minimal amount of information in the face of the millions of stimuli we constantly receive. However, this reduction of the information available to us directly influences our decisions and judgments, often leading us to erroneous or biased conclusions.

Personal filters.

Acknowledging and managing our bias is crucial to fostering a fairer, more objective, and informed decision-making process. We are all susceptible to bias; it is not an exception, but rather an integral part of our cognitive nature. Learning to recognize and manage bias is essential not only to improve our decision-making, but also to create more inclusive and equitable environments.
